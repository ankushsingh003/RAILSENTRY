[Indian Railways Permanent Way Manual (IRPWM) - Section 5: Track Maintenance]

5.1. Common Track Defects:
- Cracks in Rails: If a crack is detected, it must be reported immediately. 
- Rail Fractures: Any fracture exceeding 10mm must require immediate speed reduction to 20 km/h.
- Vibration Anomalies: Higher vibration scores (above 0.8) usually indicate loose ballast or worn-out sleepers.

5.2. Repair Procedures:
- Crack Detection: Use Ultrasonic Flaw Detection (USFD). If a defect is classified as 'IMR' (Immediate Removal), the rail must be replaced within 24 hours.
- Segment A42 Maintenance: This segment is prone to vibration due to the sharp curve. Regular packing of ballast is required.

[LHB Coach Maintenance Schedule - SOP 2024]

2.1. Wheel Overheating (Hot Box):
- Definition: Axle temperature exceeding 80°C or 20°C above ambient is considered a 'Hot Box'.
- Action: Detach the coach from the train immediately. Ensure bearing replacement at the nearest depot (e.g., Nagpur or Lucknow).

2.2. Bearing Wear:
- Signs: Audible noise and metallic vibration score > 0.7.
- Repair: Replace spherical roller bearings during IOH (Intermediate Overhaul).
